I read books (hopefully relevant to what I study) every now and again — here's the running log. I feel it's very important to read books to do with what you love to strengthen your passion. I also felt as if I need to read a tad bit more.

· · — φ — · ·
June 2026 ★★★★★★★★ 8/10
Principles of Mathematics
Bertrand Russell
March 2026 ★★★★★★★★ 8/10
Gödel's Proof (Revised Edition)
Ernest Nagel, James R. Newman, Douglas R. Hofstadter
I started reading this after receiving the results to my first ever logic module — it's safe to say it convinced me to stay on track with formal logic in philosophy.
August 2025 ★★★★★ 5/10
Number Theory
George E. Andrews
I made the stupid mistake of diving into a rather complex topic a month before starting my undergraduate studies — definitely going to re-read when I have a better understanding of Abstract Algebra.
April 2024 ★★★★★★★ 7/10
Introduction to Mathematical Philosophy
Bertrand Russell
This is the book that made me choose my degree, so it definitely holds a lot of value. I must admit I didn't understand a lot of the logical notation used on my first read.
August 2020 ★★★★★★★★ 8/10
Zero: The Biography of a Dangerous Idea
Charles Seife
When I was younger this was a book I always went back to, and one of the only books I've read to date that focuses on the history of mathematics more than rigour — which definitely made it more accessible for me at 13.
